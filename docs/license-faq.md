# ToskLight License FAQ

This FAQ explains the intent of the ToskLight Community License in practical
terms. The license itself remains the binding text.

## Why does ToskLight use this license?

ToskLight is professional lighting-control software. It is built for real
shows, venues, installations, rehearsals, schools, houses of worship, events,
and commercial productions, and it is meant to be useful at a professional
level.

The software itself is intended to be free of charge and broadly usable. The
license is designed so tinkerers, hobbyists, operators, venues, artists,
students, technicians, and integrators can run it, learn from it, modify it,
build on it, use it professionally, and get paid for installation, integration,
operation, support, and maintenance work.

The limits in the license protect a different boundary: turning ToskLight itself
into a sold software product, or selling hardware products with ToskLight
included. That kind of productization is reserved for the copyright holder or
for separately licensed partners. If ToskLight grows into a product used widely
in the field, official hardware or licensed commercial products can help fund
continued development while the software remains free to use in real
productions.

## Can I use ToskLight for a paid show, venue, or installation?

Yes. You MAY use ToskLight for professional and commercial productions.

## Can I charge a customer to install or configure ToskLight?

Yes. You MAY be paid to install, configure, integrate, operate, support, or
maintain ToskLight for someone else.

## Can I rent out a desk, controller, or server that runs ToskLight?

Yes. You MAY rent out hardware with ToskLight preinstalled.

## Can I sell ToskLight?

No. You MAY NOT sell the software itself unless you have a separate written
license from the copyright holder.

## Can I sell hardware with ToskLight preinstalled?

No. You MAY NOT sell hardware with ToskLight preinstalled, bundled, included, or
advertised as part of the hardware product unless you have a separate written
license from the copyright holder.

## Can I sell hardware separately and then install ToskLight as a service?

Yes, as long as ToskLight is not sold, bundled, included, or advertised as part
of the hardware product. The allowed paid work is the installation,
integration, operation, support, or maintenance service.

## Can I modify ToskLight privately?

Yes. Private modifications do not need to be published.

## Can I provide or install a modified version for someone else?

Yes, but if you modify ToskLight and provide, distribute, install, or otherwise
make that modified version available to someone else, you MUST visibly and
publicly publish the complete source code of that modified version under the
ToskLight Community License, at no charge.

## What does "visibly and publicly publish" mean?

The source should be easy to find from the place where the modified version is
offered, documented, or delivered. A public repository, release page, or clearly
linked source archive is appropriate. Hiding the source in an unrelated or
undisclosed location is not.

The preferred way to publish modified source is a public fork of the ToskLight
repository.

## Can I use the ToskLight name or logo for my own product?

You MAY truthfully identify the software as ToskLight, but you MAY NOT use the
ToskLight name, logos, icons, trademarks, or branding to imply endorsement,
certification, official compatibility, or partnership without written
permission.
